🕌 About Masjid Jamek Chinese Muslim Klang • Located in Bandar Botanik, Klang, this mosque officially opened on 22 August 2024 and can accommodate up to 1,000 worshippers. • The project was funded through a collaboration between the Selangor State Government, the Malaysian Chinese Muslim Association (MACMA), and public endowments (wakaf), with a total cost of approximately RM19–30 million.


🏯 Chinese-Inspired Architecture • The mosque draws inspiration from the Great Mosque of Xi’an, which is over 1,200 years old and located in Shaanxi Province, China. • It features traditional Chinese tiled roofs, a three-tiered pagoda-style minaret, courtyard layout, and classic timber structures. • The mihrab and interior walls are adorned with Chinese-style calligraphy, while the pulpit (mimbar) and wooden pillars retain delicate Chinese carvings. • The 18-meter-high minaret, known as the "Moonlight Tower", functions similarly to the pagoda tower at the Xi’an Mosque.
